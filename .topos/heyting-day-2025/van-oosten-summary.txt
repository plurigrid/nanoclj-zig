Description (by van Oosten):
"Categories and Computability" — Every logical theory gives rise to a
Lindenbaum-Tarski algebra of truth values. For classical first-order logic
these are all Boolean algebras; for intuitionistic first-order logic, all
Heyting algebras. Moving to higher-order: classically still all Boolean
algebras, but intuitionistically we do not know which Heyting algebras arise.

Key topics covered:
1. Turing's computing machines and oracles as synthetic mathematics
2. The effective topos where everything is computable
3. Hyland's embedding of Turing degrees into Lawvere-Tierney topologies
   (order-reversing, far from bijective)
4. Scott's graph model P(omega)
5. Adjunction of non-representable functions to PCAs
6. Effective operations (type-2 functionals)
7. Recent work by Takuku Kihara using game-theoretic characterization of
   oracle computation: Merlin-Arthur-Diane three-player games with bilayer
   reducibility equivalent to Lawvere-Tierney topology preorder

Q&A topics:
- Infima/suprema of Turing degrees in the topology embedding
- Comparing computational strength of different PCAs via applicative morphisms
- Universality of adjoining type-N objects
